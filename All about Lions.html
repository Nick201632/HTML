All about Lions

  • A lion is a large, social, predatory cat (Panthera leo) native to Africa and India, known for its muscular build, tufted tail, and, in males, a prominent mane.
  • Lions primarily inhabit grasslands, savannas, shrublands, and open woodlands. Historically, they ranged across Africa, Europe, and Asia, but today they are mostly found in sub-Saharan Africa and a small population in India’s Gir National Park. They are apex predators, preying mainly on medium to large ungulates
  • Lions are social animals, forming groups called prides, which consist of related females, their cubs, and one or a few unrelated adult males. Female lions usually hunt cooperatively, while males compete to maintain pride membership. Lions are generally more diurnal than other wild cats but can adapt to nocturnal activity when threatened

What Lions Eat ?

  • Lions mainly hunt medium to large ungulates, which provide enough food to sustain a pride for several days. Common prey includes zebras, wildebeests, buffalo, antelopes, gazelles, impalas, and giraffes (especially young or small individuals). In Africa, lions often target species like Grant’s gazelles, warthogs, and kudu, while Asiatic lions in India may also prey on livestock due to limited natural prey availability
