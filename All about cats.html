All about cats

  • Cats are small, domesticated carnivorous mammals known for their agility, sharp senses, and complex behaviors, forming the only domesticated species of the Felidae family.

What do cats eat

  • Cats are obligate carnivores, meaning they primarily eat meat and require a diet rich in protein and essential nutrients found in animal tissue
  • Proteins: Essential for building muscle, repairing tissues, and maintaining a healthy coat. Cats require high-quality animal proteins containing amino acids like taurine and arginine, which they cannot produce themselves
    • Fats: Provide energy and support brain, skin, and coat health.
    • Vitamins and Minerals: Especially vitamin A, B-complex, and D, which cats cannot efficiently synthesize from plants

    Cat family

    • Pantherinae (Big Cats): Includes five species of the genus Panthera (lions, tigers, leopards, jaguars, and snow leopards) and two species of Neofelis (clouded leopards). These cats are typically larger and capable of roaring due to specialized larynx structures
    • Felinae (Small Cats): Includes 34 species across 12 genera, encompassing smaller wild cats like lynxes, ocelots, caracals, and the domestic cat (Felis catus). The cheetah (Acinonyx jubatus) is also included in this subfamily . Felids are further grouped into eight lineages: Panthera, Lynx, Leopard Cat, Caracal, Bay Cat, Puma, Felis, and Ocelot lineages, reflecting evolutionary relationships among species

    Kitten

    • Kittens are juvenile cats, typically born blind, deaf, and fully dependent on their mother for survival.
