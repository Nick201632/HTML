Indicators

  • Stock market indicators are tools and metrics used to assess market trends, momentum, volatility, and overall health, helping investors make informed trading decisions.

Types of stock market indicators


  • 1. Price Indices: These track the performance of a group of stocks to reflect overall market trends. Common examples include the S&P 500, Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA), and NASDAQ Composite. They provide a snapshot of market direction and investor sentiment stockmarketsguides.com

  • 2. Technical Indicators: Technical indicators are mathematical calculations based on historical price, volume, or open interest data. They help traders identify trends, momentum, and potential reversal points. Key categories include

    • Trend Indicators: Moving Averages (SMA, EMA), Parabolic SAR, Bollinger Bands. These show the direction of the market and act as dynamic support/resistance.

    • Momentum Indicators: Relative Strength Index (RSI), MACD, Stochastic Oscillator. These measure the speed and strength of price movements to identify overbought or oversold conditions.

    • Volume Indicators: On-Balance Volume (OBV), Volume Rate of Change. These track trading activity to confirm trends or signal potential reversals.

    • Volatility Indicators: Average True Range (ATR), VIX (CBOE Volatility Index). These measure the magnitude of price swings and market uncertainty.

Indicators Names

  • A Renko chart is a price-based technical chart that uses brick-like blocks to visualize trends, filtering out minor price fluctuations and ignoring time intervals.
  • The Parabolic SAR (Stop and Reverse) is a trend-following technical indicator that signals potential trend direction and reversal points using a series of dots plotted above or below price bars.
